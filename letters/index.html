The Code That Rewired My Brain

Let me take you back to January 2025.

There I was – staring at a glass-walled room glowing like a spaceship in the middle of the Kenya National Library Service (KNLS) Little did I know... that room would become my gateway to rewriting my future. But first, I had to conquer the voice whispering:

“Places like this aren’t for people like us.”

The Spark of Curiosity

We’ve all had those moments – where curiosity crashes into self-doubt. For me, it happened everday I went for my Computer Literacy Classes at the KNLS. My Computer Literacy classmates and I would shuffle past what we called The Fancy Room – all sleek chairs and VR headsets behind glass.

“Probably for visiting experts,” we’d mutter.

“Definitely not for beginners,” I’d add.

Until one rainy Thursday, everything changed. The American Corner KNLS team threw open those glass doors:

“Why just learn computers,” they asked, “when you could create worlds?”

When Code Meets Persistence

Here’s what nobody tells you about learning to code: Your first ‘Aha!’ moment doesn’t happen at a desk. Mine came during a 5 AM shower when CSS Grid suddenly clicked. I ran out dripping, yelling:

“I GET IT!”

For 30 days, I became:

  • A detective chasing bugs through MDN Web Docs
  • An architect building HTML skeletons
  • A mad scientist embedding videos that actually played

Rewriting Mental Code

Then came the lesson that changed everything. My mentor Joseph said:

“You’re not coding websites – you’re coding persistence. Every semicolon is grit. Every responsive layout? Proof you outlasted frustration.”

Suddenly, the all-nighters made sense. lemme not even talk about that HTML table I rebuilt 17 times!! It wasn’t about borders – it was about borders I refused to accept.

Your Turn to Compile Courage

So I leave you with this challenge:

Next time you walk past a ‘fancy room’ – whether literal or metaphorical – ask not:
“Who does this belong to?”

But:
“What could I build here if I dared to walk in?”

Because sometimes...
...the most powerful code isn’t written in Python or JavaScript.

<self-doubt> → <self-belief>
